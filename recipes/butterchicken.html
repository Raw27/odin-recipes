Butter Chicken

Description

Butter chicken, traditionally known as murgh makhani, is an Indian dish originating in Delhi. It is a type of curry made from chicken with a spiced tomato and butter sauce. Its sauce is known for its rich texture. It is similar to chicken tikka masala, which uses a tomato paste.

Ingredients

  • Chicken (boneless, skinless, cut into pieces)
  • Yogurt
  • Lemon juice
  • Ginger-garlic paste
  • Ground turmeric
  • Ground cumin
  • Ground coriander
  • Garam masala
  • Chili powder
  • Salt
  • Butter
  • Heavy cream
  • Canned tomatoes (crushed or pureed)
  • Onion (finely chopped)
  • Green cardamom pods
  • Cinnamon stick
  • Cloves
  • Kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • Cooking oil

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, combine chicken pieces, yogurt, lemon juice, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, cumin, coriander, garam masala, chili powder, and salt. Mix well and marinate for at least 30 minutes, or ideally 2-3 hours in the refrigerator.
  2. Heat oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the marinated chicken pieces and cook until they are browned and cooked through, about 7-10 minutes. Remove the chicken and set aside.
  3. In the same pan, add butter and heat until melted. Add chopped onions and saut√© until golden brown, about 5-7 minutes.
  4. Add green cardamom pods, cinnamon stick, and cloves to the pan, cooking for another minute until fragrant.
  5. Stir in the crushed or pureed tomatoes and cook for 10 minutes, allowing the sauce to thicken and the flavors to combine.
  6. Add the cooked chicken back into the pan, along with any juices that have accumulated. Stir to coat the chicken with the sauce.
  7. Pour in the heavy cream and stir until the sauce becomes creamy and smooth. Let it simmer for 5-7 minutes, adjusting the seasoning with salt or additional spices as needed.
  8. Sprinkle in kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves) and mix well. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes.
  9. Serve hot, garnished with fresh cilantro, and enjoy with naan or rice!
